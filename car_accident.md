# TASK: What to do after a minor car accident

## OUTCOME

The accident is documented, reported, and submitted to insurance with required evidence.

## REQUIREMENTS

- Driver’s license.
- Vehicle registration.
- Auto insurance card.
- Phone camera.
- Pen and paper or notes app.
- Claim phone number or app for your insurer.

## STEPS

1. Move your vehicle to a safe location if drivable.
2. Turn on hazard lights.
3. Check yourself and passengers for injuries.
4. Call 911 if anyone is injured.
5. Call non-emergency police line if local law requires a report.
6. Exchange name, phone, address, and insurance details with the other driver.
7. Record the other vehicle’s license plate number.
8. Record the other vehicle’s make, model, and color.
9. Take photos of all vehicle damage.
10. Take photos of the full accident scene.
11. Take photos of street signs and lane markings.
12. Ask witnesses for name and phone number.
13. Write down the time and location of the accident.
14. Write down weather and road conditions.
15. Do not discuss fault with the other driver.
16. Notify your insurance company the same day.
17. Submit photos and notes to your insurer.
18. Request a claim number from your insurer.
19. Schedule damage inspection if your insurer requires it.
20. Save all repair estimates and receipts.

## DECISIONS

- IF the other driver leaves the scene → THEN call police and report a hit-and-run immediately.
- IF police respond → THEN request the report number before leaving.
- IF your car is unsafe to drive → THEN request roadside towing through insurance.

## FAILURE POINTS

- Leaving before exchanging information.
- Forgetting to photograph the scene.
- Admitting fault at the scene.
- Waiting too long to report to insurance.
- Losing the claim number.

## COMPLETION CHECK

- You have the other driver’s contact and insurance details.
- You have photos of damage and scene.
- Your insurer has a filed claim.
- You have a claim number.
- You know the next inspection or repair step.
