# TASK: Prepare for a doctor visit (cost-aware)

## OUTCOME

You complete a doctor visit with known expected costs and required records.

## REQUIREMENTS

- Health insurance card (if insured).
- Photo ID.
- List of current medications and doses.
- List of symptoms and start dates.
- Preferred pharmacy name and address.
- Payment method.
- Phone or computer for scheduling.

## STEPS

1. Identify the medical issue you need addressed.
2. Choose primary care, urgent care, or specialist based on severity.
3. Call the clinic to confirm they accept your insurance plan.
4. Ask for the self-pay price if you are uninsured.
5. Ask for the visit billing code range if available.
6. Ask for estimated total cost including facility fees.
7. Ask for separate lab or imaging charges.
8. Ask whether payment plans are available.
9. Schedule the appointment.
10. Ask what records to bring.
11. Request prior records be sent to the clinic if needed.
12. Write your top 3 symptoms with start dates.
13. Write your top 3 questions for the clinician.
14. List all medications, supplements, and allergies.
15. Bring insurance card and photo ID.
16. Arrive 15 minutes early.
17. Confirm your copay or upfront charge before check-in.
18. Request itemized receipt before leaving.
19. Ask when test results will be available.
20. Check the visit summary for ordered tests and follow-ups.
21. Review the bill when it arrives.
22. Compare the bill to your explanation of benefits.
23. Call billing office to dispute any incorrect charge.

## DECISIONS

- IF symptoms include chest pain, severe breathing trouble, or stroke signs → THEN go to the emergency department immediately.
- IF uninsured and cost is too high → THEN ask for community clinic or cash-discount options.
- IF a test is optional → THEN ask for price before approving it.

## FAILURE POINTS

- Not confirming insurance acceptance before scheduling.
- Not asking about separate lab or facility fees.
- Forgetting medication list.
- Leaving without an itemized receipt.
- Paying a bill without checking for errors.

## COMPLETION CHECK

- Appointment is completed.
- You received an itemized receipt.
- You know expected total charges.
- You have follow-up instructions and test timeline.
- Any billing errors are submitted for correction.
