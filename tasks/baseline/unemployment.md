# TASK: Apply for unemployment (United States)

## OUTCOME

Your unemployment claim is submitted with all required information and documents.

## REQUIREMENTS

- Social Security number.
- Government-issued photo ID.
- Mailing address and phone number.
- Employment history for the last 18 months.
- Employer names, addresses, and phone numbers for the last 18 months.
- Start and end dates for each job.
- Reason you are no longer working.
- Bank routing number and account number for direct deposit (optional).
- Work authorization document if you are not a U.S. citizen.

## STEPS

1. Find your state unemployment agency website.
2. Confirm the website ends in .gov.
3. Select the option to file a new unemployment claim.
4. Create an online account.
5. Verify your email address.
6. Sign in to your new account.
7. Start a new initial claim.
8. Enter your legal name exactly as shown on your Social Security card.
9. Enter your Social Security number.
10. Enter your date of birth.
11. Enter your mailing address.
12. Enter your phone number.
13. Enter your employment history for the last 18 months.
14. Enter your last employer’s separation reason.
15. Enter your last day worked.
16. Enter your gross earnings when asked.
17. Select direct deposit or debit card payment method.
18. Enter bank account details if you selected direct deposit.
19. Review every answer on the claim summary page.
20. Submit the claim.
21. Save the confirmation number.
22. Download or print the confirmation page.
23. Check your account inbox for identity verification requests.
24. Complete any identity verification request.
25. File weekly certification starting the first week your state allows.

## DECISIONS

- IF you worked in more than one state in the last 18 months → THEN file in the state where you last worked first.
- IF you are asked to verify identity with a third-party service → THEN complete the verification before the deadline shown.
- IF your claim is denied → THEN file an appeal using the denial notice instructions before the appeal deadline.

## FAILURE POINTS

- Entering a nickname instead of legal name.
- Entering incorrect employer dates.
- Selecting the wrong reason for separation.
- Missing an identity verification deadline.
- Not filing weekly certification after initial claim submission.

## COMPLETION CHECK

- You have a claim confirmation number.
- Your claim status shows submitted or pending in the state portal.
- You completed all requested identity verification steps.
- Your first weekly certification is filed.
