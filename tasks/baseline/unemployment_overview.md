# TASK: Understand unemployment insurance before applying

## OUTCOME

You understand what unemployment insurance is, whether you should apply now, and what will happen after you apply.

## REQUIREMENTS

- You know whether your work hours stopped, were reduced, or are still active.
- You know your last employer name and your last day worked.
- You can access your pay records and identification documents.

## STEPS

1. Confirm your current work status as stopped, reduced, or still active.
2. Identify unemployment insurance as temporary income support for people who lost work through no fault of their own.
3. Decide if your job loss situation matches a recent stop or major reduction in work.
4. Confirm basic eligibility signals: you were working recently, you lost work or hours, and you are able to work.
5. List required employer details: company name, address, phone number, start date, end date, and reason work ended.
6. List required personal details: legal name, date of birth, Social Security number or work authorization number, mailing address, phone, and email.
7. Gather supporting records: pay stubs, separation notice if you have one, and bank details for payment setup.
8. Plan application time as about 30 to 90 minutes for the first claim.
9. Plan follow-up time as about 10 to 30 minutes each week for weekly claims.
10. Submit the initial application as soon as possible after work stops or hours are reduced.
11. Check for agency messages after submission and respond to any requests for identity or work history details.
12. File a weekly claim every week while unemployed or underemployed, even if the first decision is still pending.
13. Track common delay risks: missing employer details, incomplete answers, identity verification requests, and skipped weekly claims.

## DECISIONS

- IF you recently lost your job or had a major cut in hours → THEN proceed with an unemployment application.
- IF you are still fully employed with normal hours → THEN do not apply yet.
- IF you are unsure whether you qualify → THEN apply anyway because the application determines eligibility and the risk of applying is low.

## FAILURE POINTS

- Missing employer information.
- Misunderstanding the weekly claim requirement.
- Waiting too long to apply after work stops.
- Submitting an incomplete application.

## COMPLETION CHECK

- You can answer: "Should I apply?"
- You can answer: "What do I need?"
- You can answer: "What happens next?"
